Patellar tendon rehab
Active patellar tendinopathy — lower perimeter of left knee. Do this protocol morning and evening, separate from training. Not a warm-up. Standalone rehab.
Wall sit isometric hold
Most clinically effective intervention for patellar tendinopathy. Reduces pain and stimulates tendon repair simultaneously.
Back flat against wall, thighs parallel to floor
Hold 45 seconds × 4 sets. 1 min rest between.
Do morning and evening every day until constant ache stops
Straight leg raises
Strengthens quad without loading the knee joint at all. Safe during active tendon irritation.
Lying flat, one knee bent, raise straight leg to 45°
Hold 2 seconds at top, lower slowly. 3 × 15 each leg.
Terminal knee extensions
Directly rehabilitates the tendon insertion. The most specific exercise for your location of pain.
Stand, slight bend in knee, straighten fully
Squeeze quad hard at full extension. 3 × 20. Bodyweight only.
After every session
Ice protocol and elevation. Non negotiable while constant ache is present.
Ice wrapped in cloth — 15 min on knee after training
Elevate leg above heart level for 10 min after icing
No running or jump rope until constant ache fully resolves
